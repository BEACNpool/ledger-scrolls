What is this?

A chess game that lives inside the Cardano blockchain — not on a server, not on IPFS. Its bytes are spread across transactions as a Ledger Scroll; a reader reassembled and SHA-256-verified this exact file against an on-chain manifest before letting it run. No one can edit it, delete it, or take it down. It costs nothing to read and nothing to play.

The immortal referee

The chess engine here is strictly deterministic: integer-only evaluation, fixed search depth, fixed tie-breaking, no clocks, no randomness. Given the same moves it always replies identically — in any browser, on any device, in any century. So a victory claim can be verified: replay the moves, and the engine's answers must match. The referee can never be patched, bribed, or lost.

Etching a victory

Beat the engine and you can submit a small transaction (~2 ADA, paid to an unspendable script address — nobody profits) carrying your game and an optional 64-byte message. The leaderboard replays every claim against this engine before showing a ✓. Losses cost nothing and leave no trace; every signature on the wall is a real win.

Honesty note

A claim proves the moves beat this engine at that depth — it cannot prove a human found them. That limit is fundamental, and we'd rather state it than pretend otherwise.
