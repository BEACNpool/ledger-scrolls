Recitals

WHEREAS, parties to agreements, creators of works, and custodians of records have at all times required three assurances of a written instrument — that it has not been altered (integrity), that it existed at a stated time (priority), and that an identified party stands behind it (execution) — assurances historically supplied by notaries, registries, and witnesses;

WHEREAS, a public blockchain supplies each of these assurances by mathematics rather than by institutional custody, and does so identically for every person, in every jurisdiction, at any hour, without permission;

NOW, THEREFORE, the holder of the Key declares and demonstrates as follows:

Article I — The Instrument and Its Integrity

  1. This document (the Instrument) is identified uniquely and exhaustively by the SHA-256 digest of its bytes (the Seal). The Seal is recorded inside the Instrument’s on-chain manifest at the moment of recording.
  2. Any party holding any purported copy of this Instrument may recompute its digest and compare it against the Seal. A copy whose digest matches the Seal is this Instrument, entire and unaltered, to a certainty bounded only by the collision resistance of SHA-256. A copy whose digest differs is not this Instrument.
  3. No party — including the declarant — retains any ability to amend, substitute, or withdraw the recorded Instrument.

Article II — Time of Record

  1. The Recording Transaction is incorporated in a block of the Cardano ledger bearing a slot number and timestamp fixed by the consensus of the network’s independent operators.
  2. Said timestamp establishes, against the entire world, that this Instrument existed in its exact present form no later than that moment — the function of a postmark, a notarial date, or a registered-mail receipt, performed here by a clock that no single party controls.

Article III — Execution by Cryptographic Signature

  1. The Docket is a Cardano minting policy whose terms admit exactly one signatory: the holder of the Key. No token can exist under the Docket unless the holder of the Key signed the transaction that minted it.
  2. The Recording Transaction bears an Ed25519 signature verifiable against the Key by any party, using public data alone. That signature is the execution of this Instrument: deliberate, unforgeable, and permanently affixed — the function of a wet signature and a seal, performed by mathematics.
  3. Each subsequent instrument entered under the Docket (LEGAL_0002, LEGAL_0003, and so forth) is likewise executed, creating a continuous, numbered, publicly auditable docket of records by the same signatory.

Article V — Limitations, Honestly Stated

This section is the difference between a demonstration and a deception; read it.
  1. Key custody is not legal identity. This record proves that whoever holds the Key executed this Instrument. Binding the Key to a natural or legal person requires an ordinary identity act — an affidavit, a filing, a contract clause, or public attribution maintained over time — after which every past and future record under the Docket inherits that binding.
  2. The record is public and permanent. Instruments recorded in full are readable by everyone, forever. For confidential agreements, record only the Seal: a one-page hash-anchor instrument establishes integrity, priority, and execution while the agreement itself remains private.
  3. Evidentiary weight varies by jurisdiction. Many jurisdictions recognize electronic signatures and blockchain records; their treatment is not uniform. This Instrument is a demonstrative sample, is not legal advice, and creates no rights or obligations.
  4. A lost or stolen key compromises future records, never past ones. Records already entered remain sealed by their block; a successor-key notice can be entered into the Docket as a final instrument under the old Key.

Article VI — Illustrative Applications

  • Contracts: anchor the executed agreement’s hash; both parties’ dockets record the same Seal.
  • Intellectual property: priority of authorship for manuscripts, designs, source code, research.
  • Corporate records: board resolutions and filings entered on a numbered, auditable docket.
  • Chain of custody: evidence manifests timestamped at intake, transfer, and disposition.
  • Public notices: statements that must remain exactly as issued, beyond recall or revision.
