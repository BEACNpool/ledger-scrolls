How it works — the whole protocol

A book is just an address

Any Cardano wallet is a Ledger Book. A signature is a transaction paying the protocol-minimum anchor (~1 ADA) to the book owner's address, carrying the entry in metadata label 22031. Books can be addressed by $handle — this one lives with $beacn.

Any reader can read any book

This page holds no database — it asks the chain for transactions to the book's address and lays out the pages fresh. Open any book with ?book=$handle or ?book=addr1…. Mirror this file anywhere; the books follow, because the books are the chain.

Open source, open protocol

The format is specified and MIT-licensed — build your own reader, skin it, embed it, host your book anywhere. Attribution is cryptographic: every entry is signed by its author's own wallet keys, and each card links to its transaction.
