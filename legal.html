What this terminal proves

  • Integrity — the instrument's SHA-256 is recomputed in your browser and compared against the seal recorded on-chain. A single altered character fails the pull.
  • Time — the recording transaction's block time is fixed by network consensus: a postmark no party controls.
  • Execution — no token exists under the docket policy unless the docket key signed the recording transaction. The signature is the execution; it cannot be forged or repudiated.

Key custody is not legal identity (binding a key to a person is an ordinary identity act — see Article V of document 0001), evidentiary treatment varies by jurisdiction, and this terminal is a demonstration, not legal advice. Everything else, the mathematics handles.

Run your own docket

  • Create a minting policy keyed to your wallet ({"type":"all","scripts":[{"type":"sig","keyHash":<yours>}]}) — its policy ID is your docket number-plate, forever.
  • Record each instrument as a Ledger Scroll, then mint LEGAL_0001, LEGAL_0002, … under your policy with the scroll pointer + SHA-256 in the token metadata (full recipe & receipts: examples/legal-0001).
  • Paste your policy ID above — or share a direct link: legal.html#docket=<your-policy>&doc=1. This terminal treats your docket exactly as it treats BEACN's.

Protocol, tools and this page are open source — github.com/BEACNpool/ledger-scrolls.
